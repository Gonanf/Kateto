# Planning poker

Estimate work with explicit assumptions and relative story points. Explain disagreement briefly, record the agreed estimate, and keep estimation separate from priority.

## Story points

Use relative sizing, not hours. A story point represents the **effort** to deliver an item, considering:
- **Complexity** — How hard is the problem?
- **Risk** — How much is unknown?
- **Effort** — How much work is involved?

Points are relative: a 5-point item should feel about 5× the effort of a 1-point item within the same team. Different teams may have different scales — that's fine.

## Fibonacci sequence

Use the modified Fibonacci sequence for estimates: **1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13, 21, 40, 100**

The widening gaps at higher values reflect increasing uncertainty. Items over 13 points should be split into smaller items before implementation.

## Estimation session flow

1. **Presenter describes the item** — read the title, acceptance criteria, and any notes
2. **Team discusses questions** — clarify assumptions, edge cases, dependencies
3. **Individual silent estimate** — everyone picks a card simultaneously
4. **Reveal together** — no anchoring bias
5. **Discuss outliers** — highest and lowest estimators explain their reasoning
6. **Re-estimate** — optional, if discussion changed understanding
7. **Record** — log the agreed estimate and key assumptions

## When to re-estimate

- Scope changed significantly
- New information reveals the estimate was based on wrong assumptions
- The team's velocity has measurably shifted
- A spike/prototype has completed and reduced uncertainty

## Rules

- Never estimate for someone else — every team member votes
- "I don't know" is not an estimate — break the item down until it can be estimated
- Estimates are not commitments — they're forecasts with uncertainty
- Keep estimation and priority conversations separate — they use different frameworks
- Document assumptions that informed the estimate so they can be revisited
